Multiple h1s on a page will generally NOT harm a site's placement. Unless 
really doing strange things, (I'dd say, like replacing strong and em with h1 
elements).
Matt cutts also talked about this on GoogleWebmasterHelp's youtube channel, 
he said something insane like, "unless your entire page is h1."
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GIn5qJKU8VM - More then one h1, good or bad?
http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=iR5itZlq8sk - Does the ordering of heading 
tags matter?

You could also ask yourselves this: "How long do i/we expect this 
website/design to run for?"
A lot of sites don't really change for a long time. However, search engines 
and browsers change constantly.

Don't develop your site for the search engines, focus on what works the best 
for your site/app, and what is likely to work in the future.
