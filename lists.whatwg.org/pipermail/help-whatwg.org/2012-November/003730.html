I'd agree with this one - I think it is rather unfortunate that so much
attention is paid to blogs as the 'standard' information type when
discussing html5 - it seems that every book I've seen quotes blogs as their
key examples.  It would be much better to think of the magazine article as
the typical <article>, since lengthy articles of this type often have
separately headed <sections> consisting of several paragraphs.  "Section"
is appropriate for the comments section of a blog, but a new tag <comments>
would be even more useful!

On 23 November 2012 09:50, Vahagn <vahagnh at gmail.com> wrote:

> As far as I understand, the "article" tag is for marking up self-contained
> pieces of content. In other words, content that can be taken out of context
> and distributed / reproduced elsewhere as a separate unit, with no loss of
> meaning.
>
> Wrapping each comment into it's own "article" tag seem to be wrong for
> that reason. A comment, taken out of the context of the article and other
> comments, would not represent a meaningful, self-contained piece of
> content.
>
> Here's how I'd mark up the example above:
>
>
> > <article>
> >   <h1>Cicero dictos est</h1>
> >   <p>
> >     Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur
> >     adipisicing elit,
> >     sed do eiusmod tempor incididunt ut
> >     labore et dolore magna aliqua....
> >   </p>
> >   <section>
> >     <h1>comments</h1>
>
> >       <h2>bonus est</h2>
>
> >       <p>great article;
> >        your Latin is excellent!</p>
>
> >       <h2>malus est</h2>
>
> >       <p>terrible article;
> >        you misspelled "dictus", you moron</p>
>
> >   </section>
> > </article>
>
>
> Less markup, too.
>
> / Vahagn
>
