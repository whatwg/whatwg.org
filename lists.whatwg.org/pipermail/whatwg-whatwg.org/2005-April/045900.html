Looking at http://www.whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#the-cite
I think that adopting a clearly-styled markup convention for good examples
and bad examples (like [1]) would be helpful. Someone carelessly skimming
your text should not be able to mistake an example of what /not/ to do
for an example of what /to/ do.
