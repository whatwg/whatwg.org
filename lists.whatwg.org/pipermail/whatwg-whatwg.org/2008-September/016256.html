lobbying/venting are useful forms of discussion.

> My particular beef is with the intended WebSocket support, and specifically 
> the restrictive nature of its implementation. I respectfully, yet forcefully,
>  suggest that the intended implementation is complete crap and you'd do

Nothing is engraved into stone, at least until browsers ship something and are
unable to change it because it would adversely affect their marketshare. As far
as I am aware there are currently no browser-based implementations of
WebSockets, so it is relatively easy to make changes.

> What we need (and is a really great idea) is native HTML/JavaScript support
> for Sockets - What we don't need is someone re-inventing sockets 'cos they think
> they can do it better.
