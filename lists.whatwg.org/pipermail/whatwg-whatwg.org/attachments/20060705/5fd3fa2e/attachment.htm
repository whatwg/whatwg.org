Hello,<br><br>(I didn't search the archive extensively to see if this came up before, but....)<br><br>What happened to the &quot;rev&quot; attribute?<br><br>Neither the <span style="font-family: courier new,monospace;">&lt;a&gt;
</span> or <span style="font-family: courier new,monospace;">&lt;link&gt;</span> element seems to have it anymore...<br><br><div style="margin-left: 40px;"><a href="http://whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#the-link">
http://whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#the-link</a><br><a href="http://whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#the-a">http://whatwg.org/specs/web-apps/current-work/#the-a</a><br></div><br>The &quot;rev&quot; attribute is important for allowing what I call &quot;opaque semantics&quot;.
<br><br>With &quot;opaque semantics&quot;, software can form a graph of relations without needing to understand what the labels (used in the &quot;rel&quot;, &quot;rev&quot;, and &quot;class&quot; attributes) actually mean.&nbsp; They just &quot;connect the dots&quot;, so to speak, based on matching labels.
