It's important to realize that Flash fullscreen is about a lot more than just having the video play. For many sites (such as us at YouTube), the controls/UI are part of the fullscreen experience. Unless you can fullscreen a canvas or somehow allow controls and other elements (subtitles, annotations, etc) it's simply not competitive with Flash. Right now there is no way to implement the YouTube player and features in HTML5 in fullscreen.

This thread contains an API proposal for doing all that:
